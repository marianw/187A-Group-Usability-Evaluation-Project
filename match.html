{match between system and the real world}

“The system should speak the users' language, with words, phrases and concepts familiar to the user, rather than system-oriented terms. Follow real-world conventions, making information appear in a natural and logical order.”

Explains Acronyms:
This site does a decent job at explaining acronyms. The website defines the acronyms and what they are used for, rather than leaving the user to guess what they mean.

Language Discrepancy:
The Louisiana website uses a search tool with and an option of using the “legacy” search. This term “legacy” is not a familiar word in regards to its placement as a search tool. The user has to click on it to find out what it means. After moving to the “Legacy Search” page, a large number of links are presented in a list, suggesting they are hyperlinks to other areas of the site. However, this is not the case, instead the “links” are simply text. This creates a discrepancy in the way in which we expect to interact with the text based on its format.

Overall:
This site implements language that is user-friendly, and non-technical.
