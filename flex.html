{flexibility and efficiency of use}

“Accelerators -- unseen by the novice user -- may often speed up the interaction for the expert user such that the system can cater to both inexperienced and experienced users. Allow users to tailor frequent actions.”

The Bad:

The site’s lengthy text, lack of breadcrumbs and drop-down menus make browsing on UL’s site a chore. The extensive use of links are not visually appealing- in fact, it is negatively overwhelming. Although the site does not remember individual users, the main page does allow visitors to be linked to a more customized set of links depending on their relationship with the university.

The Good:
The URL is simply http://louisiana.edu, making it very easy to remember. It is also nice that the fixed logo, which can be found at the top left corner, always links back to the main page. Despite the confusing layout of information, the site does provide a search option that can be found at the top of most of the pages, if all else fails.

Overall:
The UL website attempts to provide too much information at the expense of flexibility and ease of use. The site would be more usable if the links were better organized and navigation tools were provided to aid the user accomplish a task.
