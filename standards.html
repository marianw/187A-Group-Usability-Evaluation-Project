{consistency and standards}

“Users should not have to wonder whether different words, situations, or actions mean the same thing. Follow platform conventions..”

Different Page Styling:
The University of Louisiana has many different types of layouts and designs which branch off of the home page. For example, the “Research” page looks completely different and much more modern compared to the home page. The research page utilizes a different type of sidebar feature and the logo that usually links back to the home page no longer works. The “Library” page is also styled much differently from the home page. Rather than looking more modern similar to the "Research" page, it looks much more basic. The "Library" page has a completely different navigation bar from the homepage and there is no clear way to return to the homepage. The combination of three totally different looking pages within a single website makes it the University of Louisiana website hard to follow. This site does not follow platform conventions, as the inconsistent pages do not relate to each other and the rest of the web.


Inconsistent Navigation Headings:
The navigation headings are inconsistent as they deviate from their original labels across the different categories. For instance, of the seven categories in the main navigation bar on the homepage, only two (academics and athletics) use a sub-navigation on the secondary page. In addition, the main site uses the word “academics “ as a title on the main navigation bar, however the actual academic page uses “academic affairs” as the title. Furthermore, these inconsistencies may lead to confusing users who think they ended up in the wrong spot because the destination page had a different title from the link that took them there.


Random Highlighting:
On the “Library” page there is a section where users can see what’s new. The way that the information is initially presented as highlighted links seems arbitrarily chosen. Usually when something is highlighted or color coated it relates to another topic or category, however in this case there is no relation. The inconsistent use of highlighting makes it confusing for the user and is visually distracting, as the colors serve no purpose.
